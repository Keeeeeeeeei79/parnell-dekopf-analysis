# De Kopf — New Client Acquisition Recommendations

## Based on Parnell Customer Analysis (Stats NZ Census 2023)

---

## Executive Summary

Parnell is one of New Zealand's wealthiest suburbs, with a rapidly growing Asian population and a significant concentration of Chinese-language speakers. De Kopf is well-positioned to capture new clients by leveraging premium positioning and multilingual outreach — two strategies that are directly supported by the data.

---

## Recommendation 1: Focus on the 30–64 age group with premium services

**The data says:** Parnell's 30–64-year-olds earn a median of $86,000 — 48% more than the national median. This is not a price-sensitive market.

**What to do:**
- Lead with premium services (colour, balayage, treatments, styling packages) rather than discounts
- Position first-visit offers as "experience" incentives (e.g. complimentary treatment upgrade) rather than price cuts
- Avoid youth-oriented low-cost promotions — the 15–29 age group in Parnell actually earns less than the national average

**Why it works:** The local population has the income to support premium pricing. Competing on price in Parnell undervalues the service and attracts clients from outside the target segment.

---

## Recommendation 2: Introduce multilingual communication — especially Chinese

**The data says:** Chinese-language speakers (Mandarin + Cantonese + other Sinitic) make up over 10% of Parnell's population — more than double the national average. The Asian population has grown from 14.5% to 24.3% in just ten years.

**What to do:**
- Add Chinese-language content to the website and social media (even basic greetings and service descriptions)
- Ensure at least one team member can communicate in Mandarin or Cantonese
- Consider Chinese-language review management (e.g. responding to Google reviews in Chinese)
- Explore partnerships with local Chinese-language community groups or publications

**Why it works:** Most Parnell salons operate exclusively in English. A salon that can welcome Chinese-speaking clients in their language removes a significant barrier to first-visit booking. This is a structural competitive advantage that is difficult for competitors to replicate quickly.

---

## Recommendation 3: Optimise Google Business Profile for local search

**The data says:** This recommendation is not directly from the census data but is the highest-ROI new client channel for any local service business.

**What to do:**
- Ensure the Google Business Profile is fully completed (hours, services, photos, correct category)
- Actively request Google reviews from satisfied clients (not just Fresha reviews)
- Add posts and updates regularly to the Google Business Profile
- Target keywords like "hair salon Parnell", "colourist Parnell Auckland"

**Why it works:** New clients searching "hair salon near me" in Parnell will find De Kopf through Google before any other channel. A strong Google profile with recent reviews and complete information converts searchers into bookings at zero cost.

---

## Recommendation 4: Leverage the 65+ segment

**The data says:** Parnell's 65+ residents earn a median of $50,700 — 91% more than the national figure of $26,600. This is the most underserved high-income segment.

**What to do:**
- Consider dedicated services or time slots for mature clients (e.g. weekday mornings)
- Services such as grey blending, scalp care, and low-maintenance colour are relevant to this demographic
- This age group values personal relationships and loyalty — word-of-mouth and referral programmes are particularly effective here

**Why it works:** Many salons focus exclusively on the 25–45 demographic. Parnell's 65+ population is wealthy and underserved — a genuine gap in the market.

---

## Recommendation 5: Build a neighbourhood presence in Parnell's international community

**The data says:** French speakers (4.1% vs 1.1% nationally), Spanish speakers (3.6% vs 0.9%), and German speakers (1.5% vs 0.8%) are all overrepresented in Parnell. This is an unusually cosmopolitan suburb.

**What to do:**
- Position De Kopf as "Parnell's international salon" — a place where diverse clients feel welcome
- Consider Instagram content that reflects the neighbourhood's diversity
- Partner with nearby international cafés, restaurants, or boutiques for cross-promotion

**Why it works:** Parnell's residents come from all over the world. A salon that acknowledges and celebrates this — rather than operating as if the suburb is homogeneous — will resonate with the local community.
